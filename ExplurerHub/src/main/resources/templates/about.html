About Egypt

A crossroads of Africa and the Middle East, Egypt blends ancient civilization with vibrant modern life along the Nile and Mediterranean coasts.

This is Egypt

Egypt's story stretches back millennia: from Pharaohs and pyramids to Coptic, Islamic and contemporary heritage. Today, bustling cities like Cairo and Alexandria sit alongside desert oases, coral reefs, and timeless river towns.

Geography

The Nile Valley is Egypt's lifeline, flowing north to the Mediterranean. To the east lies the Red Sea and Sinai's rugged mountains; to the west, the Sahara's deserts and oases such as Siwa, Bahariya, and Dakhla.

History

From Old Kingdom pyramids to the temples of Luxor and Abu Simbel, Egypt's monuments trace dynasties, Hellenistic influence, Roman and Arab eras, and modern nationhood—preserved in museums and archaeological sites.

Language & Culture

Arabic is the official language, with Egyptian Arabic widely spoken. Culture spans music, film, literature, crafts, and cuisine—think koshary, feteer, and fresh seafood by the coast.
